Blazing Flow autoscaling is managed by **Blazing Core**. This page describes the behavior you can rely on as a developer without exposing internal control logic.

## What Autoscaling Does

- Scales worker capacity up when demand increases.
- Scales down during idle periods to control cost.
- Protects the system during overload by applying backpressure.

## What You Control

Autoscaling policies are configured in Blazing Core YAML. Typical controls include:

- Minimum and maximum worker counts.
- Scale‑to‑zero behavior and warm pools.
- Separate policies for trusted vs sandboxed execution.

If you need advanced tuning, use Core YAML or talk to your platform team.

## Best Practices

- Keep steps idempotent so scaling does not affect correctness.
- Use retries only when a step can be safely replayed.
- Keep cold‑start work minimal.
