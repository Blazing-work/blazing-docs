Usage alerts notify you when your spending approaches a threshold you define. This helps prevent unexpected bills by giving you early warning at 80%, 90%, and 100% of your budget.

## Setting Up an Alert

1. Go to **Settings > Organization > Billing**
2. Scroll to the **Usage Alerts** section
3. Click **Add Alert**
4. Select a **meter** (Compute Hours, Storage, or Network Egress)
5. Enter a **threshold** in USD (e.g., $500)
6. Click **Create Alert**

## How Alerts Work

When you create an alert with a $500 threshold:

| Spending Level | What Happens |
|---------------|-------------|
| **$400 (80%)** | Email notification: "Approaching threshold" |
| **$450 (90%)** | Email notification: "Approaching threshold" |
| **$500 (100%)** | Stripe billing alert triggers, email notification sent |

Alerts are checked **every 6 hours** by an automated cron job. The 100% threshold is additionally monitored by Stripe's real-time billing alert system.

## Available Meters

| Meter | What It Tracks |
|-------|---------------|
| **Compute Hours** | CPU core-hours across all deployments |
| **Storage** | Persistent storage provisioned (GiB-months) |
| **Network Egress** | Outbound data transfer (GiB) |

Each meter can have its own threshold. For example, you might set a $200 alert on Compute Hours and a $50 alert on Network Egress.

## Managing Alerts

From the Usage Alerts section on the billing page, you can:

- **View** all configured alerts with their current status (Enabled/Disabled)
- **Delete** alerts you no longer need
- **See active notifications** via the alert banner at the top of the billing page

## Alert Banner

When an alert threshold is approaching, a banner appears at the top of the billing dashboard showing:
- Which meter is approaching its threshold
- The current spending level as a percentage
- A link to manage the alert

The banner is dismissible but will reappear if spending continues to increase past the next notification level.

## Important Notes

- Alerts are **per-organization**, not per-project or per-cluster
- Notification percentages are fixed at **80%, 90%, and 100%** of the threshold
- Alerts do **not** automatically stop or scale down workloads -- they are notifications only
- The 6-hour check interval means alerts may fire slightly after the threshold is crossed
- Stripe's 100% alert fires in near-real-time
